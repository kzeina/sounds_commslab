Faiza the Falcon: In Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan

The Story

Faiza soared over Nur-Sultan, gliding alongside seagulls circling the golden Bayterek Tower. The city hummed below — modern, bold, alive. Then the rain came. She pulled out her NYU umbrella and listened to it drum against the wide stone pavements. Cold and damp, she followed the smell of roasted beans into Ministry of Coffee, wrapped her talons around a warm mug, and let the sounds of the city — wind, rain, jazz — wash over her.
